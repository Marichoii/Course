Synopsis

Ponyo is a beautifully animated fantasy film by legendary director Hayao Miyazaki. It follows the story of a magical goldfish named Ponyo who lives beneath the sea with her father, a once-human ocean sorcerer named Fujimoto. Curious about the human world, Ponyo escapes from her underwater home and ends up in a small coastal town, where she is rescued by a kind-hearted five-year-old boy named Sosuke. The two quickly form a deep bond, and Ponyo decides she wants to become human and live on land with Sosuke. However, Ponyo’s transformation is no ordinary event—it causes a dangerous imbalance in the natural world. The moon begins to draw closer to Earth, the oceans rise, and strange ancient sea creatures reawaken. As the environment shifts into chaos, Sosuke must prove the strength of his love and trust in Ponyo to restore harmony between land and sea. With its stunning hand-drawn animation, moving musical score by Joe Hisaishi, and themes of childhood innocence, environmental balance, and unconditional love, Ponyo stands as a timeless tale for audiences of all ages.

Worlds

Curiosities

Ponyo contains approximately 170,000 individual frames, and nearly all of them were drawn by hand. Hayao Miyazaki, known for his commitment to traditional animation, insisted on minimal computer graphics to maintain a tactile, organic feel. Even complex sequences—like the ocean waves or Ponyo’s transformations—were manually animated, giving the film its signature warmth and expressiveness.

In Ponyo, the sea doesn’t just serve as background—it behaves like a living being. The waves react emotionally: calm when Ponyo is at peace, violent when the balance between worlds is disturbed. This choice gives nature a central, almost spiritual presence in the film, emphasizing Miyazaki’s deep respect for the environment.

In the opening sequence, Ponyo swims with her countless little sisters—tiny magical goldfish. Each one was animated by hand, which made this one of the most time-consuming scenes to produce, but it contributes to the dreamlike quality of the film.

The story was inspired by Hans Christian Andersen’s The Little Mermaid, but Miyazaki reinterprets it through a lens of childhood innocence, environmental consciousness, and wonder. Instead of romance, Ponyo focuses on pure friendship, the harmony between humans and nature, and the beauty of transformation.

Soundtrack

Ponyo's soundtrack, composed by Joe Hisaishi, blends whimsical melodies with powerful orchestral themes. It captures the magic and emotion of the story, from playful moments to the grandeur of the sea. The theme song, “Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea”, became a hit in Japan and reflects the film’s joyful spirit.
